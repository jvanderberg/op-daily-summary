# Stay safe when cold weather strikes

**Source:** https://www.oak-park.us/News-articles/Cold-Weather
**Date:** N/A

## Summary

Oak Park officials are urging residents to take precautions as dangerously cold temperatures with below-zero wind chills are forecast for Monday, January 19, 2026. Good Shepherd Lutheran Church at 611 Randolph St. will serve as a warming center from 8 a.m. Monday through 8 a.m. Tuesday to provide shelter for those in need. The Oak Park Public Library branches, which typically function as additional warming centers, will be closed Monday in observance of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Day, and Village Hall will remain closed until 9 a.m. Tuesday.

The extreme weather poses significant health risks to Oak Park residents, particularly vulnerable populations like the elderly and those living alone. Village officials are emphasizing the importance of limiting outdoor exposure, dressing in multiple layers, and checking on neighbors who may need assistance. Residents requiring transportation to warming centers can contact the Oak Park Police Department's non-emergency line at 708.386.3800. The announcement also includes comprehensive cold weather safety guidelines, covering everything from proper clothing and recognizing hypothermia symptoms to protecting pets and preventing frozen pipes.
