# OPRF High School District 200 - 2026-02-04

Oak Park High School has two important events coming up for students planning their futures beyond graduation. All juniors are required to attend mandatory Post-Secondary Planning Meetings on February 10-11, 2026, where school counselors will guide students and families through college options, career paths, apprenticeships, and other alternatives after high school. Students must attend their assigned counselor's session during this two-day period.

Additionally, the school is hosting Black Professionals Day on February 17 from 8:50-10:50 a.m. in the Auditorium, featuring professional panels, breakout sessions, and networking opportunities to help students learn about career success. All students can participate, but must register by February 16. Both events are designed to help Oak Park students make informed decisions about their post-graduation plans during this crucial junior year period.

Oak Park High School is hosting Junior Post-Secondary Planning Meetings on Tuesday, February 10 and Wednesday, February 11, 2026. These mandatory meetings are organized by school counselors to help junior students and their families navigate post-graduation options.

The meetings will cover essential topics including tools for finding suitable colleges and career paths, information about 2-year and 4-year degree programs, apprenticeship opportunities, and other post-secondary alternatives. Students are required to attend one of their assigned counselor's sessions during the two-day period.

This initiative directly impacts Oak Park junior students and their families by providing crucial guidance during a key decision-making period in their academic careers. The meetings aim to ensure students are well-informed about their various options beyond high school graduation.

### Black Professionals Day: Tuesday, Feb. 17

The Office of Equity and Student Success is hosting Black Professionals Day on Tuesday, February 17, 2026, from 8:50-10:50 a.m. during second and third periods in the Auditorium. The event will feature professional panel discussions, breakout sessions, and networking opportunities designed to help students learn about life after high school and keys to success.

Students must register to attend the event, with registration closing on February 16. While the event appears to be targeted at connecting students with Black professionals, all students are welcome to participate. The program provides Oak Park students with valuable exposure to career guidance and mentorship opportunities from community professionals.
