### Tech Tips: 9 steps to help spot misinformation & disinformation
- **Type:** news
- **URL:** https://www.oppl.org/news-events/digital-learning/tech-tips-9-steps-to-help-spot-misinformation-disinformation/
- **Date:** January 29, 2026

This article by Oak Park's Civic Literacy Librarian Simon Czerwinskyj provides guidance on navigating today's complex media landscape and distinguishing between reliable and unreliable information sources. With another election cycle approaching, Czerwinskyj emphasizes the importance of developing strong media literacy skills to combat the spread of misinformation and disinformation on social media platforms and news outlets.

The article outlines nine key steps for improving media literacy, including considering the source's credibility, reading beyond headlines, checking author credentials, reviewing supporting sources, verifying dates and images, questioning sensational content, examining personal biases, and consulting experts. Czerwinskyj also explains the difference between misinformation (false information shared without intent to deceive) and disinformation (deliberately misleading false information).

Oak Park residents can access additional support through the library's Media Literacy Resources page and fact-checking assistance. The library staff is available to help residents with research and provide reliable sources for verification, reinforcing the institution's role as a trusted community resource for civic education and information literacy.

### Tech Tips: How to translate webpages
- **Type:** news
- **URL:** https://www.oppl.org/news-events/digital-learning/tech-tips-how-to-translate-webpages/
- **Date:** December 6, 2025

**Oak Park Public Library Provides Multilingual Web Translation Guide**

The Oak Park Public Library has published a comprehensive guide on translating web pages in multiple languages, authored by John Gargiulo, Supervising Librarian of Creative Technology. The guide, which has been translated into Spanish and Polish by library staff, provides step-by-step instructions for using translation features in four major web browsers: Google Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Microsoft Edge.

The detailed tutorial covers how to translate individual web pages, set preferred languages, and enable automatic translation features in each browser. For Chrome users, the guide explains accessing the three-dot menu and translation options, while Safari users learn to use the View menu's translation settings. Firefox and Edge users receive specific instructions for their respective browser interfaces, including how to customize language preferences and access translation pop-ups.

This multilingual resource demonstrates the Oak Park Public Library's commitment to serving its diverse community by making digital literacy accessible to Spanish and Polish speakers. The library encourages residents with technology questions to contact them directly and offers additional digital resources, including Tech Tips email subscriptions. The initiative reflects the library's broader mission to help patrons navigate and utilize technology effectively regardless of their primary language.
