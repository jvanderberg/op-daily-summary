# Connecting you to Black history, then & now

**Source:** https://www.oppl.org/news-events/equity-anti-racism/black-history-month/
**Date:** February 1, 2026

## Summary

The Oak Park Public Library is hosting a comprehensive celebration of Black History Month featuring multiple exhibits, events, and curated resources throughout February. The library is showcasing two main exhibits: "Dreamscapes by Amani Borah" in the Main Library Gallery through February 28, featuring colorful works depicting life in Mombasa and Nairobi, and "Maintaining Wonder: Deangelo & the Dandelion" in the Idea Box from January 29-February 27, an immersive installation by Chicago-based rapper and librarian Roy Kinsey that explores the interior life of a creative Black child.

The library has organized numerous events including author discussions, historical presentations, and storytelling sessions for children. Notable programming includes a presentation on "The Great Migration & The Great War" by historian Clarence Goodman on February 11, storytelling with Mama Edie McLoud Armstrong who marched with Dr. King, and multiple Rapbrary events featuring live performance and film screenings. The library is also providing extensive digital resources including curated reading lists for all ages, streaming films and documentaries, and specialized databases for African-American history and genealogy research.

This programming offers Oak Park residents diverse ways to engage with Black history and culture through art, literature, performance, and educational resources, making the celebration accessible to community members of all ages and interests.
