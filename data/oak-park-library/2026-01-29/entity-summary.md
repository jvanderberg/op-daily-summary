### Tech Tips: 9 steps to help spot misinformation & disinformation
- **Type:** news
- **URL:** https://www.oppl.org/news-events/digital-learning/tech-tips-9-steps-to-help-spot-misinformation-disinformation/
- **Date:** January 29, 2026

This article by Simon Czerwinskyj, Oak Park Public Library's Civic Literacy Librarian, focuses on helping residents develop better media literacy skills as they navigate today's complex information landscape. With another election cycle approaching, Czerwinskyj emphasizes the importance of distinguishing between misinformation (false information shared without intent to deceive) and disinformation (deliberately misleading false information).

The article provides Oak Park residents with a practical 9-step guide to improve media literacy, including evaluating sources, reading beyond headlines, checking author credentials, verifying supporting sources and images, confirming publication dates, recognizing potential satire, examining personal biases, and consulting experts. Czerwinskyj notes that the library's staff is available to assist residents with fact-checking and provides access to reliable sources and research assistance.

This guidance serves Oak Park residents by equipping them with tools to better evaluate the constant flow of information from news articles and social media posts. The library has also updated its Media Literacy Resources page and offers additional digital resources and tech support to help community members become more discerning consumers of information during this critical time.

### Curious kids welcome: Explore the world with PebbleGo & PebbleGo Next (grades K-5)
- **Type:** news
- **URL:** https://www.oppl.org/news-events/kids/curious-kids-welcome-explore-the-world-with-pebblego-pebblego-next-grades-k-5/
- **Date:** January 27, 2026

The Oak Park Public Library is promoting PebbleGo and PebbleGo Next, digital learning platforms available to local families with library cards. Children's Librarian Eileen Saam highlights these educational resources designed for students in grades K-5, which offer interactive content including articles, multimedia, games, and lessons on topics ranging from animals and science to social studies and biographies.

The platforms are tailored by age group: PebbleGo serves grades K-3 with foundational topics, while PebbleGo Next targets grades 3-5 with more advanced subjects including Indigenous peoples' history and state studies. Both resources include literacy support features such as audio narration, highlighted text, and vocabulary building tools to help children develop reading skills and digital literacy while exploring topics that match their interests and curiosity.

Oak Park families can access these educational tools immediately using their library cards, providing children with opportunities for both independent learning and school project research from home.

### Connecting you to Black history, then & now
- **Type:** news
- **URL:** https://www.oppl.org/news-events/equity-anti-racism/black-history-month/
- **Date:** January 26, 2026

Oak Park Public Library is celebrating Black History Month with a comprehensive collection of books, art exhibits, and community events running through February. The library has curated reading lists for all ages featuring titles by Black creators, along with streaming resources available through Kanopy and Hoopla that include films, documentaries, ebooks, and music.

Two major art exhibitions are currently on display: "Dreamscapes" by Amani Borah in the Main Library Gallery (through February 28), showcasing colorful scenes from Mombasa and Nairobi including fishermen, villagers, and dream-inspired fairy villages; and "Rapbrary Deangelo & the Dandelion" in the Idea Box (January 29-February 27), an immersive experience curated by Roy Kinsey featuring installations, performances, and artwork centered around the tender story of a creative Black child.

The library has scheduled multiple community events throughout February, including discussions on "A Woman of Endurance" exploring Puerto Rico's slave trade history, a presentation on the Great Migration and World War I by historian Clarence Goodman, storytelling with Mama Edie about marching with Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., and an environmental book discussion featuring "Black Earth Wisdom." Oak Park residents can also access specialized online databases for African American history research and genealogy using their library cards.
