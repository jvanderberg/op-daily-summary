# National Book Award Winners 2025

**Source:** https://www.oppl.org/news-events/authors-books/national-book-award-winners/
**Date:** November 24, 2025

## Summary

The Oak Park Public Library has highlighted the 2025 National Book Awards winners and provided read-alike suggestions curated by their librarians. The National Book Awards, established in 1950 to celebrate America's best writing, recognized five winners this year across different categories.

The 2025 winners include Rabih Alameddine's "The True True Story of Raja the Gullible (and His Mother)" for Fiction, Omar El Akkad's "One Day, Everyone Will Have Always Been Against This" for Nonfiction, Patricia Smith's "The Intentions of Thunder" for Poetry, Gabriela Cabezón Cámara's "We Are Green and Trembling" for Translated Literature, and Danile Nayeri's "The Teacher of Nomad Land: A World War II Story" for Young People's Literature.

This announcement provides Oak Park residents with an opportunity to discover acclaimed literature through both the award-winning titles and the library's recommended similar books, helping local readers explore high-quality contemporary writing across multiple genres.
