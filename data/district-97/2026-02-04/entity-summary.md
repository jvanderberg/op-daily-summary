This document outlines Oak Park Elementary School District 97's comprehensive educational transformation initiative focused on four key goals to better serve students across the district's eight elementary schools and two middle schools.

The district has announced four "Transformation Goals" that emphasize equity, excellence, and belonging: building inclusive community culture, designing instruction for all students, aligning resources strategically, and expanding learning beyond the classroom. Key initiatives include implementing a new K-5 literacy curriculum based on evidence-based reading instruction, redesigning middle school programs with better transitions and support systems, and creating the "Portrait of an Oak Park 8th Grade Graduate" framework that defines skills and qualities students should develop by graduation.

Oak Park families and residents will see impacts through new community partnerships with organizations like the Park District and Oak Park Education Foundation, enhanced safety measures through the Tri-Agency Safety Group, and more culturally responsive celebrations via the Identity and Heritage Calendar. The district is also strengthening early childhood programs and establishing a Youth Advisory Council to ensure student voices are heard in the transformation process. This comprehensive approach aims to reimagine public education while fostering creativity, social-emotional development, and a strong sense of belonging for every student.

### Board Highlights: Jan. 27, 2026
- **Type:** news
- **URL:** https://app.smore.com/n/azv9s
- **Date:** January 30, 2026

Oak Park Elementary School District 97 is conducting a comprehensive K-5 mathematics pilot program during the 2025-2026 school year as part of a multi-stage process to select a new math curriculum. The district's K-5 Math Curriculum Committee has been working to develop a new mission statement for mathematics education that emphasizes fostering number sense, hands-on learning, and a growth mindset while equipping students with critical-thinking and problem-solving skills that honor each learner's unique strengths.

Two schools, Holmes and Longfellow, are serving as pilot schools testing two evidence-based math resources: Amplify Desmos Math and Math Learning Center Bridges in Mathematics. These pilot teachers are receiving specialized coaching and training from November through March. Meanwhile, the six non-pilot schools (Beye, Hatch, Irving, Lincoln, Mann, and Whittier) are participating in professional development focused on research-based mathematics instruction strategies.

The pilot program aims to evaluate resources based on seven key criteria including alignment with Illinois Learning Standards, instructional approach, assessment capabilities, and cultural responsiveness. The district plans to gather feedback from teachers, students, and families, analyze assessment data, and make a recommendation to the Board of Education for curriculum adoption in April 2026. This initiative is designed to ensure all K-5 students have access to high-quality mathematics instruction that promotes equity and helps every student reach their mathematical potential.

Oak Park Elementary School District 97 has released its 2025-26 academic calendar, with the school year beginning on Wednesday, August 20, 2025, and ending on Tuesday, June 2, 2026. The district will hold teacher institute days on August 18-19, 2025, January 5, 2026, and March 17, 2026, during which schools will be closed to students.

The academic year will span 176 student attendance days organized into three trimesters of 58, 58, and 60 days respectively. Key breaks include Thanksgiving (November 26-28, 2025), Winter Break (December 22, 2025 - January 2, 2026), and Spring Break (March 30 - April 3, 2026). The district has scheduled two sets of family-teacher conferences: October 23-24, 2025, and February 12-13, 2026, with Thursday featuring full-day student attendance and evening conferences, while Friday will be staff-only with morning conferences.

Oak Park families should note that the district has built in five emergency days (June 3-9, 2026) if needed due to weather or other closures, and no planned absences should be scheduled until after these potential makeup days. The calendar also observes major holidays including Indigenous Peoples' Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Presidents' Day, Memorial Day, and Juneteenth, providing families with a comprehensive schedule to plan around the upcoming school year.
